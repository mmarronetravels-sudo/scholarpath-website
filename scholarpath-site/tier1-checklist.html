Why I made this

Most Tier 2 problems started as Tier 1 signals no one wrote down

In 30 years of schools — as a teacher, counselor, and administrator — I've watched the same pattern play out again and again. A student lands in Tier 2, and the team starts reconstructing a story that began months earlier: a quiet grade slip, an attendance drift, a participation change someone noticed but no one logged. By the time the data catches up, the drift has already cost the kid a semester.

Tier 1 doesn't fail loudly. It leaks. And the leaks are almost always visible long before the numbers are — if anyone is watching for them, and if the system around those teachers is set up to hear what they're seeing.

This checklist is the list I wish I'd had earlier: 10 signals across three layers — the students in front of you, the systems around them, and the paper trail behind them. Look at it with your team. If three or more feel familiar, the problem probably isn't Tier 2.
